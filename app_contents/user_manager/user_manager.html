Bangladesh-Sweden Trust Fund was established with the money received from the interest of Swedish assistance provided to Bangladesh from 1978 to 1984. The interest money was transferred to Bangladesh from Swedish Treasury.

The interest money turns into seed money which has been deposited as FDR to different commercial Banks in Bangladesh. The money is used to meet the one way travel expenses for deserving Bangladeshi students studying abroad.

It is to be noted that a policy on “Bangladesh- Sweden Trust Fund“ was approved on 07 December 2009 and circulated in Economic Relations Division’s website (www.erd.gov.bd) on 27 December 2009.


FAQ

Which label STF provides scholarship?

STF provides scholarship in under-graduate and gratuate label students who studies in different oversease universities.

How much amount STF scholarship provide?

It is not fixed for each year. It depends on STF committe's decision according to number of eligible applicants.

Who is eligible for BSTF grant?

Followings are the criteria:

  • The students must be a citizen of Bangladesh.
  • The students who have traveled abroad for higher studies at Under Graduate/ Graduate/Post Graduate/Ph.D/ Doctoral level with scholarship (full/partial) but unable to arrange travel expenditure due to financial constraints.
  • Those Bangladeshi students who have been enrolled at foreign universities/ educational institutions under scholarship programme during last academic year (specifically October-September session).
  • Those students who have not got travel grant/air ticket from Bangladesh-Sweden Trust Fund or any other source earlier.
  • Those who have not got third division/class/equivalent GPA in any public examinations.
